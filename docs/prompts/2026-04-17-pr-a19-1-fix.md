### A.1 — "Moderate 0.83% delivered is cascade fallback" — PARTIALLY INCORRECT

The evidence file §4 already corrects itself on this: Moderate hit **`phase_1_ru_max_return`** (OPTIMAL, `cvar_within_limit=True`, 7.44% ≤ 7.5%), so "cascade fallback to phase_3" explains Conservative (5.0% target, 7.36% min) and Growth (10.0% target, 10.08% min) only — not Moderate.

Why does Phase 1 with μ_max=0.4153 in the universe still deliver 0.83% objective return?

Mechanically, Phase 1 RU LP (see `quant_engine/optimizer_service.py` Phase 1 block) solves `max μᵀw s.t. RU-CVaR(w) ≤ κ, 1ᵀw=1, 0 ≤ w ≤ w_max, block caps`. With κ=7.5% **binding** (min_achievable=7.44%, slack = 6 bps), the LP has almost no feasible room to tilt toward the μ-argmax fund. It must spread weight across lower-μ, lower-tail-contribution funds to keep RU-CVaR inside 7.5%. Three compounding reasons the delivered 0.83% is low but mechanically correct:

1. **CVaR budget exhaustion.** A fund at μ=0.4153 with 1Y annualized return that high almost certainly has left-tail contribution ≳ 20% (high-vol EM / thematic / single-country ETF). Adding 1% weight to it may consume 30-50 bps of the CVaR budget. With 6 bps total slack, feasible weight on that fund is ≈ 1-2 pp at best.
2. **μ_median = 1.77%.** The rest of the universe has half its funds at μ ≤ 1.77%. A diversified low-tail basket averages to single-digit μ.
3. **Block caps + concentration limits.** Allocation blocks (fixed income, equity, alts) cap single-block weight; fixed-income block likely mandatory-weighted ≥ 30% in Moderate, with μ ≈ 2-4% for VTEB-like funds.

So 0.83% is cascade-correct **given an ill-conditioned universe**. The real pathology isn't the cascade — it's H_U1 (universe missing liquid beta) + H_U2 (μ-argmax is a data-artifact outlier). Fix those and Moderate's Phase 1 will pick SPY/IVV-heavy sleeves and deliver 6-9% E[r] at 7.5% CVaR.

### A.2 — μ-argmax fund at idx 86 / idx 75 — near-certain 1Y artifact

μ=0.4153 (cons/mod) and μ=0.5751 (growth) correspond to 1Y log-return ≈ 41% and ≈ 58%. For context:
- SPY 1Y: ~21%
- Broad EM ETFs: 5–15%
- Single-country levered / thematic (ARKK, URA, BITO, TAN): can hit 50-80%

**Hypothesis:** idx 86 is either (a) a 2x/3x leveraged ETF, (b) a crypto-tracking fund (BITO, IBIT), (c) a single-country narrow ETF benefiting from currency/tail rally, or (d) a fund with a NAV endpoint gap (stale start price from split/reverse-split not reflected in `nav_timeseries`, producing a spurious 1Y log return).

The solver never picks it in meaningful weight because its tail contribution dwarfs its μ advantage. But it distorts **universe statistics** (μ_max, μ_median, covariance conditioning) and, more importantly, it's a signal that the universe has **no quality gate on 1Y μ outliers**. Identification query in Section B below.

### A.3 — min_achievable CVaR 7.36% / 7.44% / 10.08% for 135-146 funds — ILL-CONDITIONED

For reference, a plain SPY-AGG 60/40 parametric CVaR_95 is historically **5-6%** on a 1Y daily window; an institutional all-weather portfolio with gold + IG + duration runs 3-5%. The fact that the **min-variance frontier** of a 135-fund universe bottoms at 7.36% means there is **no genuinely low-tail asset available** in the universe. This is consistent with H_U1: AGG, BND, IEF, SHY, TLT (Treasury/IG duration) are missing. Without those, the min-variance portfolio is forced onto VTEB (muni, wider spreads) + GLD (higher vol than SHY) + equity sleeves — which will not cross below ~7%.

This supports H_U1 as root cause, not just a symptom.

### A.4 — GLD 8.5% vs real 36.6% — likely window staleness, not log/simple

Log-vs-simple on 36.6% is trivial: `ln(1.366) = 31.2%`. Cannot explain 8.5% observed. A factor-of-4 gap implies **nav_timeseries data is materially stale or truncated**. Hypothesis: last price in `nav_timeseries` for GLD is several months old, so the "1Y" window is actually `(today - 1Y)` to `(last_nav_date)` which may be 9-12 months instead of 12. Or there's a missing recent month that clipped the late-period rally. Query in Section D.

### Section D — GLD window investigation (H_WINDOW, instrument-only)

**Goal:** determine whether GLD `historical_1y=8.5%` vs real-market 36.6% is caused by (a) stale `nav_timeseries` endpoint, (b) missing intermediate days, or (c) compounding convention mismatch. Ship a diagnostic; defer fix.

**D.1 — Diagnostic script** `backend/scripts/pr_a19_1_gld_window_audit.py`:

```sql
WITH gld AS (
  SELECT instrument_id FROM instruments_universe WHERE ticker='GLD' LIMIT 1
)
SELECT
  MIN(date) AS first_date,
  MAX(date) AS last_date,
  COUNT(*) AS n_days,
  MAX(date) - MIN(date) AS span_days,
  -- simple compound
  (SELECT nav FROM nav_timeseries nt JOIN gld ON nt.instrument_id=gld.instrument_id ORDER BY date DESC LIMIT 1)
   / (SELECT nav FROM nav_timeseries nt JOIN gld ON nt.instrument_id=gld.instrument_id
      WHERE date <= NOW() - INTERVAL '365 days' ORDER BY date DESC LIMIT 1)
   - 1 AS simple_compound_1y,
  -- log-sum from daily returns
  EXP(SUM(LN(nt.nav / LAG(nt.nav) OVER (ORDER BY date))) ) - 1 AS log_compound_1y
FROM nav_timeseries nt JOIN gld ON nt.instrument_id=gld.instrument_id
WHERE date >= NOW() - INTERVAL '365 days';
```
